About the Initiative

Who We Are

The Limpopo Giraffe Initiative is a registered non-profit organisation based in Limpopo Province, working within private wildlife reserves. While giraffe populations in South Africa are currently stable, they are far from secure. Fenced reserves create closed ecosystems where overpopulation becomes inevitable, and the law requires reserves to remain within their ecological carrying capacity.

The Problem

Why Giraffe Are Being Culled

Overpopulation

Reserves must remove excess giraffe to stay within carrying capacity. Smaller reserves often can't afford the costs involved.

Prohibitive Costs

Professional capture, veterinary care, safe transport, and mandatory Carrying Capacity Assessments create expenses many reserves cannot bear.

The Default

When translocation is unaffordable, the default outcome is culling — and the meat is sold. Giraffe deserve better than becoming a commodity.

Our Solution

Relocation, Not Extermination

  • Fund professional translocation of giraffe from overstocked reserves
  • Support ecological Carrying Capacity Assessments
  • Enable smaller reserves to participate in conservation
  • Improve genetic diversity across populations
  • Prevent culling as the default management tool

Our Approach

Short-Term and Long-Term Strategy

Short Term

Relocate giraffe within South Africa — moving animals from overstocked reserves to those with capacity, preventing immediate culling and strengthening local populations.

Long Term

Establish a regional sourcing centre for neighbouring countries, enabling cross-border conservation partnerships and expanding the reach of ethical giraffe management across southern Africa.

Proven Example

Blyde to Mountainlands

In a landmark operation, 11 giraffe were successfully relocated from Blyde Wildlife Reserve to Mountainlands Nature Reserve near Barberton. This translocation improved genetic diversity, strengthened both populations, and prevented the culling of healthy animals. It is living proof that the model works.
