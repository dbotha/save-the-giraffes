About Us

The Limpopo Giraffe Initiative is a registered NPO based in Limpopo Province, operating within private wildlife reserves. While the giraffe population is currently stable, it is far from secure. Fenced reserves restrict natural migration, leading to overpopulation. South African law requires reserves to comply with carrying capacity limits — and that means excess giraffe must be removed.

The Problem

Reserves must remove excess giraffe to remain within legal carrying capacity. But for smaller reserves, the costs are simply too high.

Cost 1

Capture

Professional wildlife capture is expensive and requires specialist teams.

Cost 2

Veterinary Care

Each giraffe needs thorough veterinary checks before and after relocation.

Cost 3

Transport

Specialised vehicles and logistics for safe long-distance giraffe transport.

Cost 4

Assessments

Carrying capacity assessments must be completed by qualified ecologists.

The Default?

When reserves cannot afford relocation, the default is culling — and selling the meat.

Our Solution

  • Fund translocation of giraffe from overstocked reserves
  • Support carrying capacity assessments for smaller reserves
  • Enable smaller reserves to receive giraffe they otherwise could not afford
  • Improve genetic diversity across fragmented populations
  • Prevent culling as the default management tool

Our Approach

Short Term

Relocate Within South Africa

Move excess giraffe from overstocked reserves to suitable destinations across South Africa, funding capture, veterinary care, and transport.

Long Term

Regional Sourcing Centre

Develop Limpopo as a regional sourcing centre for giraffe needed in neighbouring countries, contributing to broader conservation across southern Africa.

Proven Example

Blyde to Mountainlands

From Blyde Wildlife Reserve, 11 giraffe were successfully translocated to Mountainlands Nature Reserve near Barberton. This operation improved genetic diversity, strengthened both populations, and prevented culling. It proves the model works — and it can be replicated.
