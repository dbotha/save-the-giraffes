ABOUT US

WHO WE ARE

LGI is a registered NPO in Limpopo Province, working within private wildlife reserves. The giraffe population here is stable but not secure. Fenced reserves prevent natural dispersal and lead to overpopulation. South African law requires landowners to comply with carrying capacity limits — and when they cannot afford relocation, culling becomes the default.

THE PROBLEM

WHY GIRAFFE ARE CULLED

THE COST BARRIER

Reserves must remove excess giraffe. Smaller reserves cannot afford capture teams, veterinary care, or specialised transport. They also need a Carrying Capacity Assessment before any animal can move.

THE DEFAULT OUTCOME

When relocation is unaffordable, the default response is culling and selling the meat. It is legal. But it does not need to be the only option.

RELOCATE WITHIN SOUTH AFRICA

Move giraffe from reserves that exceed carrying capacity to properties with space and suitable habitat within the country.

LONG TERM

REGIONAL SOURCING CENTRE

Establish a sourcing centre for neighbouring countries that need giraffe for restocking and conservation programmes across southern Africa.

PROVEN EXAMPLE

BLYDE TO MOUNTAINLANDS

From Blyde Wildlife Reserve, 11 giraffe were successfully translocated to Mountainlands Nature Reserve near Barberton. This single operation improved genetic diversity, strengthened both populations, and prevented culling. The model works — and it can be repeated.
