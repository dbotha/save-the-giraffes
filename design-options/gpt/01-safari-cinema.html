About Us

Who We Are

The Limpopo Giraffe Initiative (LGI) is a registered Non-Profit Organisation based in the Limpopo Province of South Africa, working directly within private wildlife reserves.

South Africa has a relatively stable population of southern giraffe — but this does not mean the population is secure. Most giraffe live on fenced private reserves, where they cannot roam freely. Over time, this inevitably leads to local overpopulation, which must be carefully managed to prevent environmental degradation and animal suffering.

By law, reserve owners are required to keep wildlife numbers within the land’s carrying capacity, as determined by a formal assessment.

The Crisis

The Problem

Many reserves need to remove excess giraffe to remain compliant with regulations. However, smaller reserves and community conservation areas cannot afford the high costs of translocation:

  • Capture
  • Veterinary care
  • Transport

Receiving reserves must complete a Carrying Capacity Assessment.

Without a viable alternative:

Culling the excess animals and selling the meat.

What We Do

Our Solution

LGI raises funds to make giraffe relocation possible — preventing unnecessary culling across South Africa.

01

Full Translocation Costs

Cover the full cost of giraffe translocation, including fair compensation to the source reserve.

02

Assessment Support

Support Carrying Capacity Assessments to ensure ecological sustainability at receiving reserves.

03

Enable Smaller Reserves

Enable smaller reserves to receive giraffe that they otherwise could not afford to translocate.

04

Genetic Diversity

Improve genetic diversity across populations by strategically moving animals between reserves.

05

Prevent Unnecessary Culling

Every giraffe we relocate is one saved from being culled. We provide reserves with a humane, conservation-driven alternative.

Strategy

Our Approach

Short Term

Relocating Within South Africa

Relocating giraffe within South Africa — preventing culling while strengthening local populations across private and community reserves.

Long Term

Regional Sourcing Centre

Establishing a regional sourcing centre, facilitating movement to neighbouring countries and expanding conservation impact across southern Africa.

Proof of Impact

A Proven Example

At Blyde Wildlife Reserve, we successfully translocated 11 giraffe to Mountainlands Nature Reserve near Barberton, Mpumalanga.

  • Improved genetic diversity across both reserves
  • Strengthened both populations for long-term sustainability
  • Prevented culling of healthy animals
