Registered NPO

The Limpopo Giraffe Initiative operates within private wildlife reserves in Limpopo Province, South Africa.

Stable but Not Secure

The giraffe population is currently stable, but fenced reserves restrict natural migration and lead to overpopulation.

Legal Mandate

South African law requires carrying capacity compliance — excess giraffe must be removed from overstocked reserves.

About the Initiative

LGI is a registered non-profit organisation working on the ground in Limpopo Province. We operate within private wildlife reserves where fenced boundaries prevent natural giraffe migration. Without intervention, overpopulation becomes inevitable — and the law demands compliance with carrying capacity.

The Problem

Reserves must remove excess giraffe. For smaller reserves, the costs of humane removal are simply unaffordable.

Capture

Professional wildlife capture requires specialist teams and equipment most small reserves cannot afford.

Veterinary Care

Each giraffe needs thorough veterinary checks before and after translocation.

Transport

Specialised vehicles and logistics are needed for safe long-distance giraffe transport.

Carrying Capacity Assessment

Qualified ecologists must assess both source and destination reserves.

The Default?

When reserves cannot afford relocation, the default is culling — and selling the meat.

Our Solution

Fund Translocation

Cover the costs of moving giraffe from overstocked reserves to new homes.

Support Assessments

Pay for carrying capacity assessments at smaller reserves.

Enable Smaller Reserves

Help reserves that want giraffe but cannot afford the logistics.

Genetic Diversity

Improve gene flow across fragmented, fenced populations.

Prevent Culling

Replace culling as the default management tool with funded relocation.

Our Approach

Short Term

Relocate Within South Africa

Move excess giraffe from overstocked reserves to suitable destinations across the country, funding every step from capture to release.

Long Term

Regional Sourcing Centre

Develop Limpopo as a regional sourcing centre for giraffe needed in neighbouring countries, contributing to continental conservation efforts.

Proven Example

Blyde to Mountainlands

From Blyde Wildlife Reserve, 11 giraffe were successfully translocated to Mountainlands Nature Reserve near Barberton. This operation improved genetic diversity, strengthened both populations, and prevented culling. It is proof that the model works — and it can be replicated across the region.
