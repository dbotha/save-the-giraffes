Chapter 1 — About Us

Who We Are

LGI is a registered NPO in Limpopo Province, working within private wildlife reserves. While the giraffe population here is stable, it is not secure. Fenced reserves create artificial boundaries that lead to overpopulation, and South African law requires landowners to comply with carrying capacity limits.

Chapter 2 — The Context

Stable but Not Secure

Fenced boundaries prevent natural dispersal. As herds grow, reserves exceed carrying capacity. Without intervention, landowners face legal obligations that often end in culling — the cheapest and most accessible option available.

Chapter 3 — The Problem

Why Giraffe Are Being Culled

The Cost Barrier

Reserves must remove excess giraffe to stay within legal limits. Smaller reserves simply cannot afford the capture teams, veterinary care, and specialised transport required for safe relocation.

Assessment Requirements

Before any animal can move, a qualified Carrying Capacity Assessment must be completed for both sending and receiving properties. These assessments are expensive and time-consuming.

The Default: Culling

When relocation is too expensive, the default outcome is culling and selling the meat. This is legal, but it does not need to be the only option.

Chapter 4 — Our Solution

Relocation, Not Culling

  • • Fund translocation of giraffe from overstocked reserves
  • • Support carrying capacity assessments for sending and receiving sites
  • • Enable smaller reserves to participate in ethical wildlife management
  • • Improve genetic diversity across fragmented populations
  • • Prevent unnecessary culling wherever possible

Chapter 5 — Our Approach

Short-Term and Long-Term Goals

Short Term

Relocate giraffe within South Africa — moving animals from reserves that exceed carrying capacity to properties with space and suitable habitat.

Long Term

Establish a regional sourcing centre for neighbouring countries that need giraffe for restocking and conservation programmes across southern Africa.

Chapter 6 — Proven Example

Blyde to Mountainlands

From Blyde Wildlife Reserve, 11 giraffe were successfully translocated to Mountainlands Nature Reserve near Barberton. This single operation improved genetic diversity, strengthened both populations, and prevented culling. It proves the model works — and that it can be repeated.
