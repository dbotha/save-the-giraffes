Who We Are

The Limpopo Giraffe Initiative (LGI) is a registered Non-Profit Organisation based in the Limpopo Province of South Africa, working directly within private wildlife reserves.

South Africa has a relatively stable population of southern giraffe — but this does not mean the population is secure. Most giraffe live on fenced private reserves, where they cannot roam freely. Over time, this inevitably leads to local overpopulation, which must be carefully managed to prevent environmental degradation and animal suffering.

By law, reserve owners are required to keep wildlife numbers within the land's carrying capacity, as determined by a formal assessment. When giraffe numbers exceed what the land can sustainably support, reserves are forced to reduce their herds.

The Problem

Many reserves need to remove excess giraffe to remain compliant with regulations. However, smaller reserves and community conservation areas — which would gladly receive these animals — cannot afford the high costs of translocation, including:

Capture

Veterinary Care

Transport

In addition, receiving reserves must complete a Carrying Capacity Assessment to determine how many giraffe their land can sustainably support.

Without a viable alternative, the giving reserve is often left with only one option: culling the excess animals and selling the meat to recover financial value.

Our Solution

LGI is changing this. As a locally based non-profit, we raise funds to:

Full Translocation Funding

Cover the full cost of giraffe translocation, including fair compensation to the giving reserve.

Carrying Capacity Support

Support receiving reserves with the required Carrying Capacity Assessment process.

Enable Smaller Reserves

Enable smaller reserves and community projects to receive giraffe they otherwise could not afford.

Genetic Diversity

Improve genetic diversity across fenced populations through carefully planned relocations.

Prevent Unnecessary Culling

By funding the entire translocation process, we eliminate the need for reserves to resort to culling as the only financially viable option.

Our Approach

Short Term

We focus on relocating giraffe within South Africa — preventing culling while strengthening and balancing local populations.

Long Term

We aim to become a regional sourcing centre. From our base in Limpopo, we will expand our impact by facilitating the movement of surplus giraffe to neighbouring countries — helping restore populations and expand habitat connectivity across borders.

A Proven Example

At Blyde Wildlife Reserve in Limpopo, we have first-hand experience in this. Recently, we successfully translocated 11 giraffe to Mountainlands Nature Reserve near Barberton, Mpumalanga. We had more, but could not find reserves with the funds to receive them.
