About the Initiative

The Limpopo Giraffe Initiative (LGI) is a registered Non-Profit Organisation based in the Limpopo Province of South Africa, working directly within private wildlife reserves.

South Africa has a relatively stable population of southern giraffe — but this does not mean the population is secure. Most giraffe live on fenced private reserves, where they cannot roam freely. Over time, this inevitably leads to local overpopulation, which must be carefully managed to prevent environmental degradation and animal suffering.

By law, reserve owners are required to keep wildlife numbers within the land's carrying capacity, as determined by a formal assessment. When giraffe numbers exceed what the land can sustainably support, reserves are forced to reduce their herds.

"In the Lowveld, every life matters."

The Problem

Why giraffe face an invisible crisis on private reserves

Many reserves need to remove excess giraffe to remain compliant with regulations. However, smaller reserves and community conservation areas — which would gladly receive these animals — cannot afford the high costs of translocation, including:

Capture

Specialist capture teams, equipment, and darting by experienced wildlife vets.

Veterinary Care

Pre- and post-translocation veterinary monitoring and health checks.

Transport

Specialised vehicles and logistics for safely moving tall, fragile animals.

In addition, receiving reserves must complete a Carrying Capacity Assessment. Without a viable alternative, reserves resort to culling the excess animals and selling the meat to recover financial value.

Our Solution

LGI is changing this

Full Translocation Costs

Cover the full cost of giraffe translocation, including fair compensation to source reserves.

Carrying Capacity Support

Support receiving reserves with Carrying Capacity Assessments required by law.

Enable Smaller Reserves

Enable smaller reserves and community conservation areas to receive giraffe they could never otherwise afford.

Genetic Diversity

Improve genetic diversity across isolated populations by moving animals between reserves.

Prevent Unnecessary Culling

Every giraffe we relocate is a giraffe saved from being culled. We provide a real, humane alternative.

Our Approach

From local action to regional impact

Short Term

Local Relocations

Relocating giraffe within South Africa — preventing culling while strengthening local populations across fenced private reserves that need them most.

Long Term

Regional Hub

Establishing a regional sourcing centre from our Limpopo base, facilitating the movement of giraffe to neighbouring countries and expanding conservation impact across southern Africa.

A Proven Example

The Blyde Wildlife Reserve relocation story

Chapter One

Overpopulation at Blyde

At Blyde Wildlife Reserve, giraffe numbers exceeded the land's carrying capacity. The reserve faced a difficult decision: reduce the herd or risk environmental degradation and animal suffering.

Chapter Two

Finding a New Home

Rather than resorting to culling, LGI identified Mountainlands Nature Reserve near Barberton, Mpumalanga, as an ideal destination — with suitable habitat and capacity for new giraffe.

Chapter Three

The Translocation

Eleven giraffe were successfully captured, veterinary-checked, and safely transported from Blyde to Mountainlands — a carefully coordinated operation involving specialist teams.

Chapter Four

A Success Story

The move improved genetic diversity, strengthened both populations, and — most importantly — prevented culling. With your support, many more relocations like this are possible.
